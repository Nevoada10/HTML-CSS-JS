Layne Staley

  1. In 1967 - Born on August 22 in Kirkland, Washington, near the birthplace of grunge music.
  2. In 1987 - Forms Alice in Chains, a band that would go on to revolutionize the music industry with their unique blend of hard rock, grunge, and heavy metal.
  3. In 1990 - Releases Facelift, their debut album, which features hits like "Man in the Box" and establishes them as a force in the music scene.
  4. In 1992 - Releases Sap, a critically acclaimed acoustic EP that showcases the band's versatility and emotional depth.
  5. In 1992 - Releases Dirt, their second full-length album, which addresses themes of addiction and despair. It becomes their most successful album, selling over 5 million copies in the U.S. alone.
  6. In 1996 - Releases their third studio album, Alice in Chains (also known as the "Tripod" album), which continues to explore darker themes and receives critical acclaim.
  7. In 2002 - Tragically passes away on April 5 at the age of 34 due to a drug overdose, leaving behind a profound musical legacy.

Alice in Chains: Legacy After Layne

Though Layne Staley's passing was a profound loss, Alice in Chains continued to honor his memory:

  • In 2009 - Released Black Gives Way to Blue, their first album with new lead vocalist William DuVall, featuring a tribute to Layne.
  • In 2013 - Released The Devil Put Dinosaurs Here, continuing the band's musical evolution while preserving their distinctive sound.
